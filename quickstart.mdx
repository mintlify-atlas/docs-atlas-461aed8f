This guide walks you through the minimum steps to get Clash Verge Rev routing traffic after a fresh installation: importing a subscription profile, enabling a proxy mode, and verifying that your connection works. The whole process takes under five minutes.

<Note>
  You need a valid **Clash Meta-compatible subscription URL** from your proxy provider before you begin. This URL points to a YAML or JSON configuration file that contains your proxy servers and routing rules. Contact your provider if you do not have one.
</Note>

## Setup steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install Clash Verge Rev">
    If you have not installed the app yet, follow the [installation guide](/installation) for your operating system, then return here.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Profiles page">
    Launch Clash Verge Rev. In the left sidebar, click **Profiles**. This is where you manage your proxy configuration files (subscriptions).

    <Info>
      A **profile** (also called a subscription) is a configuration file provided by your proxy service. It defines proxy servers, routing rules, and other settings in a format compatible with Clash Meta. Clash Verge Rev downloads and caches it locally.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Import a subscription URL">
    1. Click the **Import** button (or paste your URL into the URL input field at the top of the Profiles page).
    2. Paste your Clash Meta-compatible subscription URL into the field.
    3. Click **Import** to download and save the profile.

    Clash Verge Rev will fetch the remote configuration, validate it, and add it to your profile list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Activate the profile">
    Click on the profile card you just imported to select it. The active profile is highlighted. Clash Verge Rev will apply its proxy servers and rules immediately.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable system proxy or TUN mode">
    Choose one of the two proxy modes depending on your needs:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="System proxy">
        In the left sidebar, click the **System Proxy** toggle (or navigate to **Settings** and enable **System Proxy**).

        System proxy mode configures your operating system's HTTP/HTTPS proxy settings to route browser and most application traffic through Clash Verge Rev.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="TUN mode">
        Navigate to **Settings** and enable **TUN Mode**.

        TUN mode creates a virtual network adapter that routes **all** outgoing traffic — including applications that do not respect system proxy settings — through the Mihomo core. This mode requires elevated permissions and is best for full-device proxying.

        <Warning>
          TUN mode requires administrator or root privileges. The app will prompt you for permission when you enable it for the first time.
        </Warning>
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify your connection">
    Open a browser and visit a site you know your proxy should reach. If the page loads correctly, your setup is working.

    You can also check the **Connections** page in Clash Verge Rev to see live traffic flowing through the proxy, with matched rules displayed for each connection.

    <Tip>
      To check proxy server latency, go to the **Proxies** page, find your proxy group, and click the latency test button (the speedometer icon) next to a node. Nodes are color-coded by response time, making it easy to pick the fastest one.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
